Cost basis
Net price is the DoE-reported average after grants and institutional aid — usually the most realistic figure for planning.

Net price

What students actually paid on average after grants and scholarships directly from the school — free money, not loans. Reported by the DoE from FAFSA data. Usually the most realistic number for planning.

e.g. Sticker tuition $58k → net price $28k after school aid

In-state tuition

The sticker price for residents of that state, before any aid. Public universities charge residents less because state taxpayers subsidize it. Does not include room & board in the DoE figure — we add room & board on top.

e.g. U Michigan in-state $17k/yr vs out-of-state $55k/yr

Out-of-state tuition

Sticker price for non-residents. Often 2–3× the in-state rate at public universities. Private universities have no in/out distinction — everyone pays the same sticker price.

Rule of thumb: net price is usually the right choice unless you know your exact residency situation
